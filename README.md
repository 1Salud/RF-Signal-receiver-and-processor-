# RF-Signal-receiver-and-processor-
Collaborative project between Hlib Panfilov and Salud Avila in comprising an RF signal receiver and down converter front end, and embedded signal processing backend 
This allows one person to own the Analog/RF Hardware domain while the other owns the Digital/Embedded Firmware domain—perfectly mimicking how real-world engineering teams at companies like Tesla or Apple operate.Expanded Scope: The Embedded SDR Receiver PlatformInstead of outputting a simple analog Intermediate Frequency (IF) to an external scope, you add a high-speed Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC) and an STM32 microcontroller directly onto the board. The goal becomes capturing a real-world wireless signal, digitizing it, and processing it via basic DSP algorithms.Division of LaborHere is the cleanest way to split the project so you aren't stepping on each other's toes, allowing both of you to maximize your resume impact:

Partner A: The RF & Analog Hardware EngineerThis partner is responsible for getting the wireless signal from the air down to a clean, digitizable analog voltage.Responsibilities:Designing the $50\ \Omega$ RF input matching network and Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) stage.Setting up the Local Oscillator (LO) PLL/Synthesizer and mixer circuit to step the signal down to a low IF.Designing the active anti-aliasing low-pass filter to match the input range of the ADC.Altium Focus: Layer stack management, controlled-impedance RF routing, via fences, and isolating the noisy power supplies from the sensitive RF path.

Partner B: The Mixed-Signal & Firmware EngineerThis partner is responsible for the digital architecture of the board, getting the data off the ADC, and handling the initial signal processing.Responsibilities:Selecting and integrating a high-speed ADC (e.g., a SPI-based or parallel ADC) capable of sampling the IF signal.Integrating an STM32 microcontroller (or similar) onto the schematic to act as the central host controller.Designing the digital power rails, clock distribution networks, and breaking out debugging pins (JTAG/SWD, UART).Altium & Firmware Focus: Routing high-speed digital buses, managing signal integrity on clock lines, and writing the initial firmware (using DMA and SPI/Parallel interfaces) to pull samples into a memory buffer without stalling the processor.

How to Collaborate Effortlessly in AltiumWorking on the same PCB can be tricky, but you can use professional collaboration strategies:Modular Schematic Sheets: Altium allows you to create hierarchical schematics. Partner A can completely own the "RF_FrontEnd.SchDoc" sheet, while Partner B owns the "MCU_Digital.SchDoc" sheet. You connect them using top-level sheet entries (ports).Layout Partitioning: When it comes to the PCB layout, split the board visually down the middle. Partner A places and routes the RF side, keeping all components tight and linear. Partner B handles the MCU micro-architecture, routing the high-pin-count digital lines and power distribution networks.Use GitHub/GitLab: Treat your Altium project like software. Commit your schematic changes and layout milestones to a shared Git repository so you always have a working backup and clear version control.
